# Small-n Management

**Small-n Management** is about the part of leadership where the usual comfort of averages disappears: four strategic customers, six enterprise deals, three critical suppliers, one irreplaceable hire, or a single regulatory decision that can change the future of the business.

The main thesis is simple: when the denominator is small, variance dominates signal. A dashboard can look precise while telling the wrong story, because one event can move the metric by 25%, 50%, or more. In those environments, the useful question is not "What is the trend?" but "Which specific event happened, why did it happen, and what does it reveal about the system?"

The book argues that small-n management is not a smaller version of large-company management. Large-n environments reward optimization, forecasting, experimentation, and statistical confidence. Small-n environments reward resilience, causal reasoning, redundancy, scenario planning, and careful attention to named relationships and irreversible decisions.

It is written for leaders operating in high-stakes, low-volume contexts: enterprise sales, strategic accounts, founder-led companies, specialized teams, executive hiring, supplier concentration, and any situation where a single event can reshape the whole operating picture.

The PDF lays out the practical operating system: how to diagnose when you are in small-n territory, when statistics remain useful and when they mislead, how to reason causally from sparse evidence, how to size risk when you cannot diversify, and how to build the buffers and protocols that let a small system survive shocks.
